# LeResearch.org — Funding Pipeline 2026

This corpus is the working list of US-eligible grants that **require 501(c)(3) status, fiscal sponsorship, or an academic / institutional administering organization** — meaning they belong to LeResearch.org's funding pipeline rather than LeDesign AI LLC's. Sister file: `LeDesign-ai/grants/funding-pipeline-2026.md` (which holds for-profit-applicable grants).

**Status of LeResearch.org:** in formation as of 2026-04-30. Nothing in this pipeline is applyable until at minimum the 501(c)(3) determination letter is in hand (or a fiscal-sponsor relationship is signed with an existing 501c3, e.g. Code for Science & Society or PPF). A few entries also require Ruby to have completed her PhD before she can be the named PI; those are flagged.

**Hard constraints on this pipeline:**
- US-eligible only. International / Chilean grants belong to a separate Chilean-entity pipeline, not here.
- 501(c)(3) status (or fiscal sponsor) is the dominant unlock — ~7 of the 9 entries below gate on it.
- Academic-PI grants (Spencer, William T. Grant) gate on Ruby's PhD completion (expected 2026/2027).
- Nothing was fabricated; cross-checked with sponsor URLs as of 2026-04-30. `[VERIFY 2026 STATUS]` flags items where the cycle status is materially uncertain.

## 2. Academic / research-affiliated grants (PI-driven)

These foundations historically require an academic PI with an earned doctorate working through a non-profit administering organization. LeResearch.org (501c3) can be the administering org once incorporated; Ruby can be the PI once she has her PhD (expected 2026/2027).

- **Sponsor:** The Spencer Foundation
- **Amount:** Up to $50,000 over 1–5 years
- **Cycle / Deadline:** Two cycles per year; FY26 small-grants full proposals due **April 15, 2026** (closed); next round typically late summer 2026 `[VERIFY 2026 STATUS]`
- **US-eligible:** Yes
- **Stage fit:** **PI must hold an earned doctorate AND be affiliated with a non-profit or public institution that serves as administering organization.**
- **LeResearch fit (1–5):** 4 once Ruby has her PhD AND LeResearch.org has 501c3 status (LeResearch can serve as the administering organization)

- **Notes:** This is the cleanest path for Ruby to do an evaluation study of LeDesign's early-childhood device once she defends her dissertation. Park until 2026/2027.

## 3. Open-source / research org grants (nonprofit-favored)

- **Sponsor:** Alfred P. Sloan Foundation
- **Amount:** Varies; OSPO grants $650K–$700K+ historically; smaller exploratory grants under $200K
- **Cycle / Deadline:** Rolling — submit a 2-page LOI to opensource@sloan.org
- **US-eligible:** Yes
- **Stage fit:** Strongly favors academic / nonprofit applicants. LeResearch.org as a 501c3 fits cleanly; LeDesign LLC was structurally an unusual fit.
- **LeResearch fit (1–5):** 4 — open-source-in-science is a natural framing for LeResearch's open-methodology work; the Chilean legal-AI corpus (LeJustice's 18K+ normas, currently in LeDesign) could be reframed as an open-data scholarly resource via LeResearch
- **Application complexity:** Low for LOI; high if invited
- **Required materials beyond standard pitch:** 2-page LOI
- **URL:** https://sloan.org/programs/digital-technology/open-source-in-science
- **Notes:** Send a 2-page LOI as a low-cost probe once LeResearch.org has a clear research thesis. Frame around open-data legal corpora and open-methodology educational research, not the LLC business itself.
